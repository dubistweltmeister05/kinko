# Hydrogen Generation Plant Monitoring & Control System

## Initial Understanding and Requirements Summary

### Purpose of this Document

This document summarizes the current understanding of the customer's hydrogen generation system and identifies what they are asking us to develop.

The goal is not to finalize the design, but to establish a clear picture of the overall system before making hardware, software, or costing decisions.

---

# 1. System Overview

The customer appears to be developing a decentralized hydrogen generation and distribution system.

The broad idea is:

* Generate hydrogen centrally at a village-scale plant.
* Store hydrogen locally.
* Distribute hydrogen to households for cooking and other uses.

The plant is powered primarily by solar energy.

## High-Level System Flow

```text
Solar PV Arrays
       ↓
      MPPT
       ↓
 Battery / DC Bus
       ↓
  Electrolyser
       ↓
 Hydrogen Storage
       ↓
 Distribution Network
       ↓
 Household Users
```

---

# 2. What is an Electrolyser?

An electrolyser uses electrical energy to split water into hydrogen and oxygen.

```text
Water + Electricity
          ↓
 Hydrogen + Oxygen
```

Inputs:

* Water
* Electrical energy

Outputs:

* Hydrogen gas
* Oxygen gas

The electrolyser requires continuous monitoring for safe and efficient operation.

Typical measurements include:

* Voltage
* Current
* Temperature
* Pressure
* Electrolyte level
* Electrolyte concentration

---

# 3. What is Balance of Plant (BoP)?

The BoP includes all supporting equipment required for the electrolyser to function.

Examples:

* Pumps
* Valves
* Cooling systems
* Water circulation systems
* Filters
* Safety equipment
* Instrumentation

Think of the electrolyser as the core machine and the BoP as everything required to keep it operating safely.

---

# 4. Hydrogen Storage

Hydrogen generated by the electrolyser is stored in tanks before use.

Storage systems require monitoring of:

* Pressure
* Temperature
* Safety valve status
* Tank levels

Hydrogen storage is a critical safety component of the plant.

---

# 5. Solar Power System

The plant is intended to operate from solar energy.

## Components

### Solar PV Arrays

Generate DC electrical power.

### MPPT Controllers

MPPT stands for Maximum Power Point Tracker.

Each MPPT optimizes energy extraction from a solar array.

```text
Solar Array
      ↓
    MPPT
      ↓
   DC Bus
```

Multiple MPPTs may feed a common DC bus.

The customer expects:

* Up to 7 solar arrays per monitoring board
* Expansion in multiples of 7
* Example: 7, 14, 21 arrays

---

# 6. DC Bus

The outputs of multiple MPPTs are connected together to form a common DC bus.

```text
MPPT 1 ──┐
MPPT 2 ──┼── DC Bus
MPPT 3 ──┘
```

Customer notes indicate:

* Current should be measured individually for each MPPT.
* DC bus voltage only needs to be measured once.
* The same DC bus voltage is available to the electrolyser.

# 8. Proposed Controller Structure

The customer's requirements naturally separate into three controller categories.

---

## Board A: Electrolyser + BoP + Storage Controller

### Purpose

Monitor and control the hydrogen generation plant.

### Inputs

Analog:

* Voltage
* Current
* Temperature
* Pressure
* Level sensors
* Electrolyte concentration (TBD)

Most sensors are expected to provide:

* 4–20 mA outputs

Some sensors may be digital.

### Outputs

* Solenoid valves
* Relays
* Alarm outputs
* Hooters

### Spare Capacity

Customer requested:

* 2–3 spare analog channels
* 2–3 spare digital channels

---

## Board B: Solar + Battery Monitoring Board

### Purpose

Monitor solar power generation and energy storage.

### Inputs

Per MPPT:

* Voltage
* Current

Plant-wide:

* Solar irradiance sensor
* DC bus voltage
* Battery voltage
* Battery current

### Expansion

One board supports:

* Up to 7 MPPTs

Multiple boards should be able to communicate together.

---

## Board C: Household / Kitchen Controller (Future)

Not currently urgent.

### Functions

Monitor:

* Hydrogen leakage
* Flow rate
* Main valve status

Potential communication method:

* LoRa

Further investigation required.
